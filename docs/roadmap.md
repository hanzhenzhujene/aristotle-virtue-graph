# Roadmap

The current product is intentionally narrow:
it is a fully reviewed, passage-grounded explorer for *Nicomachean Ethics* Book II.

The next meaningful expansion is not “more ontology.”
It is more Aristotle, added in books that deepen the graph's explanatory value.
Book II establishes the method and the shape of the ethical field, but by itself it is still
too thin to become a lasting reference tool.
The right next move is to expand in the books that complete the argument, not to pile more UI
or schema onto the existing slice.

## Why these books

The strongest next sequence is:

1. Book III
2. Book IV
3. Book VI
4. Book X

That order follows Aristotle's own payoff curve for this project:

- Book III completes what Book II only introduces around courage, temperance, voluntary action,
  and choice.
- Book IV supplies the richest additional run of virtue/vice/domain triads.
- Book VI explains how practical judgment actually finds the mean.
- Book X gives the system an endpoint in happiness and contemplation.

## Book III

Book III would complete what Book II only sketches about:

- courage in full argumentative depth
- temperance in fuller detail
- voluntary action, responsibility, and choice

Why it matters:
users who open `courage` in Book II currently meet the triad and its supporting passage,
but not Aristotle's fuller argument for why courage has that shape.
Book III would let the graph move from outline to fuller case.
It would also turn `choice` and `voluntary action` from supporting nodes into concepts that can
actually be explored on their own argumentative terrain.

## Book IV

Book IV would enrich the graph's middle band of practical virtues:

- generosity and magnificence
- pride
- truthfulness
- wit
- friendliness

Why it matters:
these virtues are especially graph-friendly.
They create a dense, auditable set of virtue/vice/domain triads that would make the network
more useful for students, reading groups, and downstream dataset work.
Book II already hints at this structure; Book IV is where it becomes extensive enough to feel
like a reference map rather than a small demonstration set.

## Book VI

Book VI would add the intellectual hinge of the ethical system:

- practical wisdom (`phronesis`)
- deliberation
- right reason in fuller form

Why it matters:
Book II says the mean is determined by right reason and the practically wise person,
but Book VI is where Aristotle explains what that actually amounts to.
Adding it would connect the graph's descriptive structure to Aristotle's account of judgment.
Without Book VI, the current app can show what the mean looks like in Book II, but not yet how
Aristotle thinks a person actually finds it.

## Book X

Book X would give the project a stronger endpoint:

- happiness
- pleasure revisited
- contemplation and the best life

Why it matters:
many users come to Aristotle with the question, “What does the good life finally look like?”
Book X is where the graph could answer that directly rather than leaving the ethical system open.
It would also let the project circle back to pleasure in a fuller way, showing how one of Book
II's formative signals fits into Aristotle's broader account of flourishing.

## Who that expansion serves

This expansion changes the answer to “who is this for?”

- With Book II alone, the project is strongest as a focused Book II explorer, assignment aid,
  and methodological proof of concept.
- With Books II, III, IV, VI, and X together, it becomes a much stronger companion for reading
  the *Nicomachean Ethics* as a whole.
- That broader shape would serve students, reading groups, self-directed readers, and
  researchers who want auditable philosophical graph data rather than loose summaries.

## What stays fixed

Even as the corpus grows, the rules should remain the same:

- passage-grounded concepts and relations
- clear separation between textual claims, editorial normalization, and interpretation
- no database requirement
- no chatbot layer
- stable ids once exported
